Answer-First Intro

Building a hotel in Texas in 2026 costs between $150 and $600+ per square foot, depending on brand tier, market, and finish level. A limited-service property in a secondary Texas market typically runs $8M–$18M all-in; a full-service urban hotel can exceed $80M. Labor shortages in the DFW and Houston metro corridors and ongoing material cost volatility are the primary cost drivers this year.

TL;DR / Key Takeaways

  • Hard costs range from ~$150/SF (limited-service, economy) to $450+/SF (full-service, upscale) in Texas markets.
  • Soft costs (design, permits, FF&E, OS&E, pre-opening) add 20–30% on top of hard costs.
  • DFW, Austin, and Houston carry a 10–15% labor premium over secondary Texas markets.
  • Ground-up construction timelines run 18–36 months from permit to punch-list for most flag types.
  • Use a Guaranteed Maximum Price (GMP) contract to cap exposure on material escalation.

What Are the Main Cost Categories for a Texas Hotel Build?

Every hotel project budget breaks into four buckets: hard costs, soft costs, FF&E/OS&E, and financing/pre-opening.

Hard Costs

Hard costs cover site work, foundation, structural, MEP (mechanical, electrical, plumbing), and finishes. In Texas, concrete and structural steel costs remain elevated from 2024–2025 tariff cycles.

Soft Costs

Soft costs typically run 20–30% of hard costs and include:

  • Architecture and engineering (typically 8–12% of hard costs)
  • Permits and impact fees (varies widely by municipality — Austin and Austin-area municipalities have historically added $500K–$2M in fees for larger projects)
  • Pre-construction and project management
  • Legal, title, and financing fees

FF&E and OS&E

Furniture, Fixtures & Equipment plus Operating Supplies & Equipment add $8,000–$35,000 per key depending on brand and tier. Brand flags (Marriott, Hilton, IHG) impose specific FF&E standards with approved vendor lists.

Financing and Pre-Opening

Interest carry during construction, pre-opening staffing, and working capital typically add another 5–10% to total project cost for a standard 24-month build.

What Drives Hotel Construction Cost Overruns in Texas?

The three most common causes of cost overruns on Texas hotel projects are scope creep, permitting delays, and subcontractor availability gaps.

Scope creep is the leading driver. Late changes to room count, brand upgrade requirements, or owner program additions after GMP execution are costly. Lock scope before finalizing the GMP.

Permitting delays in high-growth Texas markets (Austin, Round Rock, Kyle) routinely add 3–6 months to project schedules, compounding interest carry.

Subcontractor depth is thin in secondary markets. Specialty trades — commercial kitchen, elevator, curtain wall — may require importing crews, which inflates both cost and schedule risk.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to build a 100-room hotel in Texas?

A 100-room select-service hotel in Texas in 2026 costs approximately $18M–$28M in total project cost, assuming ~65,000 SF of gross floor area, current Texas labor rates, and a standard midscale flag. Urban markets (Austin, DFW) will skew toward the top of that range; secondary markets toward the lower end.

How long does hotel construction take in Texas?

Most hotel projects in Texas take 18–30 months from permit issuance to certificate of occupancy. Full-service and luxury projects in urban markets can run 30–42 months when permitting time is included. Adding pre-design and entitlement, total development timelines of 36–48 months are common.

What brand flags are most cost-efficient in Texas?

Select-service flags from Marriott (Fairfield, Courtyard), Hilton (Hampton Inn, Tru), and IHG (Holiday Inn Express) are the most cost-efficient for Texas markets in 2026. Soft brands and independent boutiques can save 5–10% in FF&E by avoiding brand-mandated vendors, but require stronger management infrastructure.

What is the difference between hard cost and total project cost?

Hard cost is the construction contract value — labor and materials for the physical structure. Total project cost adds soft costs, FF&E/OS&E, land, financing carry, and pre-opening expenses. For most Texas hotel projects, total project cost runs 1.35–1.55x the hard cost.
