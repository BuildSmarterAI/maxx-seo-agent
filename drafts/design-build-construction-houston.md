## What Is Design-Build Construction?

Design-build is a project delivery model where the owner signs one contract with a single entity — the design-builder — who is responsible for both the design and the construction of a project. This is the direct alternative to **design-bid-build** (DBB), where the owner separately contracts an architect, then puts the completed drawings out for contractor bids.

The legal and contractual distinction matters in Texas: under the Texas Government Code (Chapter 2269 for public projects), design-build is a recognized alternative delivery method. For private commercial projects, the model is unrestricted — any licensed GC who employs or subcontracts licensed design professionals can offer design-build delivery.

## What Types of Projects Benefit Most from Design-Build in Houston?

### Is design-build right for every commercial project?

Design-build delivers the most value in projects where:

- The owner has a **clear program and budget** but not completed construction documents.
- The format is **repeatable or prototype-driven** (QSR, strip retail, flex warehouse, medical office suites).
- **Speed to revenue is the primary constraint** — every week of construction is a week of lost rent or sales.
- The owner has **limited internal project management capacity** and wants a single point of contact.

Design-bid-build remains the better choice when:
- The owner has a **complex custom program** requiring independent design review (historic renovation, high-rise, complex MEP-heavy healthcare).
- The owner wants to **competitively bid** a complete set of documents to multiple contractors.
- A **lender or public agency requires** separate architect-of-record with independent oversight.

## How Does the Design-Build Process Work Step by Step?

### What are the phases of a design-build project?

A typical Houston commercial design-build project follows six phases:

1. **Owner brief and program** (1–2 weeks): Owner defines project type, site, budget, and functional requirements. Maxx Builders issues a preliminary budget range within 5 business days of receiving a site address and program.

2. **Schematic design and GMP** (2–4 weeks): The integrated team produces schematic drawings sufficient to lock a Guaranteed Maximum Price (GMP) or lump-sum contract. Price risk transfers to the design-builder at this point.

3. **Design development and permitting** (3–8 weeks, concurrent with early procurement): Design is developed to permit-ready level while the GC procures long-lead items (steel, MEP equipment). This concurrency is the primary source of schedule compression.

4. **Construction** (8–22 weeks depending on scope): Field work proceeds with the same team that designed the project — RFIs and design conflicts resolve faster because the architect is in-house.

5. **Closeout and CO** (2–4 weeks): Certificate of Occupancy, commissioning, punch list, and as-built documentation.

6. **Post-occupancy** (optional, 12 months): Warranty period with single-contract accountability.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is design-build more expensive than design-bid-build?

Design-build is typically 5–12% less expensive on a total project cost basis, primarily because integrated teams resolve design conflicts before they become construction change orders. Change orders in design-bid-build projects average 8–15% of contract value in Houston commercial construction; in design-build, that figure drops to 3–6% in comparable project types. ### Does Texas require a special license for design-build?

Texas does not issue a separate "design-build license." The GC must hold a valid Texas contractor registration and must employ or subcontract licensed engineers (PE) and architects (AIA/RA) for the appropriate disciplines. Maxx Builders maintains licensed design professionals on staff and as exclusive subcontractors. ### How do I know if a Houston contractor is truly a design-builder?

Ask for the names and Texas license numbers of the architects and engineers who will be on your project, and verify them against the Texas Board of Architectural Examiners (TBAE) and the Texas Board of Professional Engineers (TBPE) registries. A GC who "provides design-build" by simply referring you to an independent architect is not actually a design-builder — that is still design-bid-build with extra steps.

### Can design-build work for tenant improvement projects?

Yes. Design-build TI projects are common in Houston for national retail and restaurant chains executing prototype fit-outs across multiple sites. The efficiency gain is proportionally smaller on small TI scopes (under $500K) but the single-contract accountability benefit applies at any scale.

### What is a GMP contract in design-build?

A Guaranteed Maximum Price (GMP) contract caps the owner's cost at an agreed ceiling while allowing the design-builder to share savings if the project comes in under budget. GMP contracts are typical in design-build projects over $1M in Houston. For smaller scopes, lump-sum (fixed-price) contracts are more common because the design is sufficiently defined at contract execution.
