# San Antonio Commercial Construction Cost Per Square Foot (2026)

*By [Harris Khan, Founder — Maxx Builders](https://www.linkedin.com/in/maxxbuildersharris/) · Last updated July 7, 2026*

**Commercial construction in San Antonio costs roughly $135–$430 per square foot in 2026** — typically about 5% below the Dallas–Fort Worth (DFW) baseline, making it one of the more cost-favorable major Texas metros to build in. Warehouse and light-industrial shells anchor the low end; restaurants, medical offices, and high-finish retail and office push toward the top. San Antonio's softer labor market relative to Austin and DFW is the primary reason its per-square-foot pricing runs slightly lower than the other big-four Texas markets.

- **Typical range:** $135–$430/sq ft for new commercial construction in San Antonio (2026).

## How Does San Antonio Compare to Austin and DFW?

**San Antonio is the cost-favorable corner of the Texas Triangle — it generally builds cheaper than both Austin and DFW.** The driver is the labor market: Austin's construction demand runs hot and pushes its per-square-foot costs above the state average, while San Antonio's deeper, less-overheated trade pool keeps pricing closer to — and slightly below — the DFW baseline.

| Texas Metro | Cost Posture vs. DFW Baseline | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Austin | +8% to +12% | Overheated demand, tight labor, fast growth |
| Houston | +5% to +8% | Large market, complex sites, industrial demand |
| Dallas–Fort Worth | Baseline | Deepest, most balanced subcontractor pool |
| San Antonio | ≈ −5% | Softer labor market, lower cost of doing business |

For owners deciding between Central Texas markets, the San Antonio-vs-Austin gap is real: a project that pencils tight in Austin may have meaningfully more room roughly 80 miles south.

## What Drives San Antonio Commercial Construction Costs?

### Structural System Choice

The frame is the first major cost fork. Tilt-up concrete is the Texas workhorse for warehouse, retail, and many office buildings. Pre-engineered metal buildings (PEMBs) are cheaper for simple industrial spans but constrain bay sizing. Structural steel costs more but is required for tall, irregular, or crane-served buildings. Use dictates the right system, not budget alone.

### MEP and Finish Level

Mechanical, electrical, and plumbing — plus interior finish — are where building *type* becomes building *cost*. A warehouse needs minimal MEP; a restaurant or clinic carries heavy kitchen exhaust, grease interceptors, medical gas, or specialized HVAC. That is why a restaurant can cost three to four times a warehouse per square foot.

### Site and Civil Work

Site work is the most underestimated budget line. Grading, detention, utility extensions, and paving can swing total cost by $10–$40/sq ft. San Antonio's hill-country geology can introduce rock excavation on some sites — a real cost and schedule risk worth confirming with geotechnical work early.

### 2026 Material and Labor Escalation

Texas remains one of the busiest nonresidential construction markets in the country. San Antonio's labor market is less overheated than Austin's, but skilled trades are still in demand, and material pricing (steel, concrete, glazing, switchgear) remains volatile with extended lead times on electrical gear and rooftop units.

### Delivery Model

How you contract the work affects total cost as much as what you build. Design-build and CMAR let the builder price and value-engineer during design, cutting change-order risk. See [design-build construction](https://www.maxxbuilders.com/design-build-construction-houston/) for how the model controls cost — the same principles apply in San Antonio.

## How Long Does Commercial Permitting Take in San Antonio?

**Commercial permitting in San Antonio is handled by the City's Development Services Department (DSD), and review time typically runs from several weeks to a few months depending on scope, submittal completeness, and current backlog.** Permitting is schedule, and schedule is carrying cost — so it belongs in every honest budget conversation.

The most reliable way to shorten review is a complete, coordinated submittal — a preconstruction discipline, not luck. Front-loading code review and agency coordination during design protects the schedule far more effectively than expediting later.

## How Do San Antonio Construction Costs Break Down by Category?

A rule-of-thumb cost breakdown for a mid-spec San Antonio commercial building:

| Cost Category | % of Total Construction Cost |
|---|---|
| Structural / shell (foundation, frame, roof, walls) | 30–45% |
| Site work and civil / utilities | 10–20% |
| MEP (mechanical, electrical, plumbing) | 15–25% |
| Interior finish / tenant improvement | 10–20% |
| Fire/life-safety (sprinkler, alarm) | 3–6% |
| General conditions, fees, contingency | 10–15% |

The MEP and finish bands shift substantially between a warehouse and a restaurant or clinic — the higher the mechanical and finish demand, the larger those two categories grow as a share of total cost.

## How Can You Control Commercial Construction Costs in San Antonio?

**The cheapest savings happen before construction starts.** Value engineering during design — not during construction — is the most effective cost-control lever. Three moves matter most:

1. **Engage the builder early (preconstruction).** Real-time pricing during design catches budget-busters while they are still cheap to fix on paper — including San Antonio-specific risks like rock excavation.
2. **Choose the right delivery model.** Design-build and CMAR consolidate accountability and reduce the change-order risk that inflates lump-sum jobs.
3. **Carry honest contingency.** Incomplete-design estimates need more contingency, not less.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does it cost to build a commercial building in San Antonio in 2026?

Commercial construction in San Antonio costs roughly **$135–$430 per square foot** in 2026, depending on building type and finish — about 5% below the DFW baseline. ### Is it cheaper to build in San Antonio than in Austin?

Generally, yes. San Antonio typically builds for less than Austin because its construction labor market is less overheated. Retail and strip-center construction in San Antonio generally runs **$150–$285 per square foot**, driven by façade complexity, glazing, and the level of tenant white-box finish included in the base building.

### How long does commercial permitting take in San Antonio?

San Antonio commercial permitting is handled by the Development Services Department (DSD) and typically takes from several weeks to a few months, depending on project scope, submittal completeness, and department workload. A complete, coordinated submittal is the most reliable way to shorten review.

### What contingency should I budget for a San Antonio commercial project?

Budget **10–15% contingency** with complete construction documents, and **20–25%** on early-stage estimates with incomplete design. In San Antonio specifically, early geotechnical investigation helps quantify rock-excavation risk before it becomes a change order.
