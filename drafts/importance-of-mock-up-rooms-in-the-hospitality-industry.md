## Answer-First Intro

A mock-up room is a single, fully built guest room constructed before the rest of the hotel to test brand standards, finishes, ADA compliance, and FF&E placement. It gives the construction team — and the brand's representative — one room to review and approve so that all downstream rooms replicate a confirmed design rather than an untested one. Catching one wiring error in a mock-up costs a fraction of what it costs across 120 rooms.

## TL;DR / Key Takeaways

- A mock-up room is built in two stages: rough (structural systems exposed) then finished (brand standards applied).
- Most national hotel brands (Marriott, Hilton, Hyatt, IHG) require a brand-approved mock-up before full construction proceeds.
- Skipping mock-up or building it late forces expensive rework across every guest room.
- ADA-compliant accessible mock-up rooms must be built alongside standard room types — grab bar placement and turning radius cannot be corrected after framing is closed.
- Order FF&E for the mock-up only at first; do not commit full furniture orders until the brand approves the room.

## What Is a Mock-Up Room in Hotel Construction?

A mock-up room is the single-room prototype a hotel project team uses to validate design, systems, and brand compliance before replicating those decisions across every guest room in the building. It serves as the physical contract between the owner, the construction manager, and the brand: once approved, it defines the standard every other room must match.

Mock-up rooms matter because hotel projects involve high repetition — often 80 to 300+ identical or near-identical guest rooms. An undetected error in the template multiplies across every room. A mock-up room limits exposure to one room rather than the entire project.

## The Two-Stage Mock-Up Process

### Stage 1: Rough Mock-Up Room

The rough mock-up is built with the structural framework exposed. Walls are framed but not closed, which allows the project team to resolve critical systems questions before they are buried behind drywall:

- **Electrical outlet placement and elevation** — verify ADA-compliant heights, convenience outlet clustering, and USB/data locations match the FF&E plan.
- **Plumbing rough-in** — confirm fixture locations align with the room layout and brand requirements.
- **Ceiling light placement** — test spacing, alignment with furniture, and coordination with sprinkler head locations.
- **Backing for wall-mounted items** — TVs, mirrors, artwork, and ADA grab bars all require backing. Rough mock-up is the only cost-effective time to confirm backing location and load rating.
- **3D furniture mockups** — wood-stick or foam-core stand-ins for beds, desks, nightstands, and chairs let the team confirm clearances and traffic flow without committing to actual furniture.

On Maxx Builders hotel projects, the brand's regional construction director is invited to review the rough mock-up at MEP rough-in stage for Hilton-flagged properties. For IHG properties, the area construction manager typically reviews the finished mock-up only. The rough-stage walk is scheduled as a formal milestone on the project schedule, not an informal site visit.

### Stage 2: Finished Mock-Up Room

The finished mock-up applies the full brand prototype package: flooring, wall coverings, paint, FF&E, appliances, window treatments, linens, and artwork. This is the stage where the brand manager or brand representative conducts a formal inspection.

Brand representatives verify:

- Color palettes match the approved brand standard
- FF&E matches the brand's approved product list (substitutions require variance approval)
- Signage, collateral, and brand elements are properly placed
- MEP finishes (light fixtures, outlet covers, HVAC registers) are brand-compliant
- ADA-compliant room elements (grab bars, turning radius, accessible fixtures) meet requirements

Once the brand approves the finished mock-up, the construction team is authorized to replicate the design throughout all guest rooms. The approved mock-up room effectively becomes the job standard — referenced in punchlist reviews, QC walks, and close-out documentation.

## Mock-Up Room vs. No Mock-Up: Cost and Schedule Impact

| Factor | With Mock-Up | Without Mock-Up |
|---|---|---|
| Design errors discovered | In 1 room, pre-commit | Across all rooms, during or after construction |
| Brand approval timing | Before full framing closes | Risk of late brand rejection — full rework |
| FF&E order risk | Validate before bulk order | Full order at risk if design changes |
| ADA rework exposure | Caught before wall close | Expensive structural correction |
| Schedule impact of a change | Days, in one room | Weeks, across the floor plate |
| Typical mock-up schedule | 3–5 weeks | N/A — saved time becomes rework time |

The industry consensus: the cost of building a mock-up room (labor and materials for one room, plus 3–5 weeks of schedule) is consistently less than the cost of correcting a single discovered-late error across 100+ rooms.

## ADA Requirements in Hotel Mock-Up Rooms

ADA compliance in hotel guest rooms is not optional and not easily corrected after framing closes. The rough mock-up stage is the mandatory checkpoint for accessible room elements:

- **Grab bars**: Required at toilet, tub, and shower. Backing must be installed during framing; location and height are regulated under ADA Standards for Accessible Design §§609.
- **Turning radius**: 60-inch clear floor space for wheelchair turns — verify in mock-up before furniture is specified.
- **Accessible route**: Door clear width (minimum 32 inches clear), threshold height, and hardware type must be confirmed.
- **Fixture heights**: Outlets, switches, thermostats, and controls have regulated reach-range limits (15–48 inches AFF).

On Maxx Builders hotel projects, ADA accessible room review is handled internally by the project manager and superintendent using the construction documents and ADA Standards for Accessible Design as the reference. A third-party accessibility consultant is engaged when the owner's lender or brand specifically requires one as a pre-funding deliverable.

## Timing: When to Build the Mock-Up

Timing the mock-up is a critical-path decision, not an afterthought.

**Order materials for the mock-up only first.** Do not place full FF&E orders before the mock-up is brand-approved. If the brand requires a substitution or layout change, early bulk ordering creates sunk cost and potential re-procurement delays.

**Build the mock-up during early structural work.** Sequencing the mock-up while the rest of the building is still in structural framing means brand feedback can be incorporated without disrupting the main construction schedule. Maxx Builders sequences mock-up early as a deliberate critical-path strategy on hotel projects.

**Account for international lead times.** FF&E specified from overseas manufacturers (common for brand-standard furniture) carries 12–20+ week lead times depending on origin country, shipping mode, and seasonal factors including national holidays. These lead times must be built into the schedule before the mock-up is started, not after it is approved.

## How Many Mock-Up Rooms Do Hotels Typically Build?

Most hotel projects build one mock-up room per room type offered:

- Standard king
- Double queen
- Extended-stay suite
- ADA accessible room (required in each type)

Room dimensions vary even within the same type. A 150-room hotel may have 60 different king-size rooms with dimensional variations driven by the building's structural grid. Building 2–3 mock-up rooms with representative dimensional variations is enough to establish a design solution that scales to all dimensional variants. Each room type and dimension variant should be documented in updated drawings after mock-up approval.

## What Does a Brand Representative Check During the Mock-Up Inspection?

The brand rep — typically a regional construction director or brand compliance manager — arrives with the brand's PIP (Property Improvement Plan) and brand standards bible. They check:

1. All finish samples match the approved finish schedule — flooring, wall covering, case goods, soft goods, window treatment.
2. All fixture specifications match brand-approved vendor SKUs — plumbing trim, lighting, HVAC cover plates.
3. ADA accessible room compliance — turning radius, grab bar placement per §609, knee clearance under vanity, roll-in shower vs. tub specification.
4. Technology rough-ins — USB port locations, TV mounting height, in-room control system wiring.
5. MEP coordination at the unit level — PTAC placement, bath exhaust, fire sprinkler head type and location.

The inspection produces a punch list; items must be corrected before the brand will authorize replication across all room types. GC responsibility: maintain an updated finish binder and coordinate the inspection date at least three weeks in advance through the franchise development contact.

## How Much Does a Hotel Mock-Up Room Cost?

The mock-up room is a real cost line item that should appear in the GMP, not be hidden inside general conditions. Typical cost breakdown for a single mock-up room in Texas, based on Maxx Builders project experience and current market conditions:

- **Framing and drywall** (beyond standard construction, adds for additional finishes and corrections): $2,500–$5,000
- **Finish installation** (flooring, wall covering, millwork): $8,000–$15,000 depending on brand tier
- **FF&E procurement for one room**: $12,000–$25,000 for mid-scale; $20,000–$45,000 for upscale — purchased before the rest of the project to enable inspection
- **Brand rep travel and scheduling coordination**: $500–$2,500
- **Correction and remediation after inspection**: $3,000–$12,000 on a first-time project; less on repeat GCs who know the brand

**Total mock-up investment: $25,000–$60,000 for a select-service room.** Compare that to the cost of a single error replicated 120 times: a wrong light fixture at $400/unit × 120 rooms = $48,000 in remediation plus potential brand variance fee. The mock-up pays for itself on every project.

## Brand-Specific Mock-Up Requirements: Hilton, Marriott, IHG, and Hyatt

**Hilton (Hampton Inn, Tru, Garden Inn):** Two-stage inspection standard — rough mock-up at MEP rough-in and finish mock-up at substantial completion of the unit. Regional construction director sign-off required before mass replication. Hampton allows limited regional modifications with approval.

**Marriott (Courtyard, Fairfield, AC Hotels):** Single finish mock-up standard for most select-service. Marriott Design Excellence team reviews before approval. Variance process for non-prototype markets requires written approval from brand VP.

**IHG (Holiday Inn Express, Candlewood, Staybridge):** One inspection with the area construction manager. IHG standards are generally the most flexible for regional material substitutions with approved equivalents.

**Hyatt (Hyatt Place, Hyatt House):** Similar to IHG in flexibility. Two-stage is required only for full-service Hyatt properties.

Maxx Builders has direct experience with Hilton and IHG brand compliance on completed Texas projects — Home2Suites and Comfort Suites brand inspections completed without major remediation on either project.

## Common Mock-Up Room Mistakes and How Maxx Builders Avoids Them

Five recurring errors in hotel mock-up execution:

1. **Ordering full-quantity FF&E before brand approval** — if the brand requires a substitution, the owner has purchased non-compliant product. Correct approach: order mock-up quantity only, get approval, then purchase the balance.

2. **Scheduling the mock-up inspection after framing is fully complete on multiple floors** — at that point, MEP rough-in errors are expensive to correct. Schedule the rough mock-up inspection early, while other floors are still in structural framing.

3. **Missing ADA room type in the mock-up** — brands require an ADA accessible room in the mock-up set, not just the standard room.

4. **Omitting structural backing from drywall for TV mounts, grab bars, and wall-hung millwork** — discovered at inspection, correction requires opening finished walls. Backing schedule must be confirmed at rough mock-up, not assumed.

5. **Not updating contract drawings to reflect mock-up approval** — the approved mock-up should become the reference document for QC walks throughout construction. Undocumented changes get re-introduced by subcontractors changing shifts.

## Mock-Up Closeout Documentation: What You Need Before Brand Sign-Off

The documentation package required to formally close the mock-up inspection and authorize full replication:

1. **Updated finish schedule** reflecting any approved substitutions, signed by the brand rep.
2. **As-built drawings for the mock-up unit** showing final MEP locations, backing schedule, and millwork dimensions.
3. **Approved FF&E cut sheets** for all installed items — the GC maintains this as the reference spec for the remainder of the project.
4. **ADA compliance photo set** — turning radius documentation, grab bar placement photos with dimensions.
5. **Brand variance approvals in writing** for any deviations from prototype.
6. **Punch list with sign-off dates.**

This package becomes the QC reference for every room walk during construction. On projects where this package is incomplete, brand representatives have required partial re-inspections at owner expense — a risk that is entirely preventable with proper closeout documentation discipline.

## FAQ

### What is the purpose of a mock-up room in hotel construction?

A mock-up room is a fully constructed single guest room used to validate design decisions, brand standards, and system placements before those decisions are replicated across the entire hotel. It limits the cost of errors to one room and gives the brand representative a physical room to inspect and approve.

### How long does it take to build a hotel mock-up room?

Most hotel mock-up rooms take 3–5 weeks to construct from rough framing through brand-approved finish. The timeline depends on FF&E lead times, brand inspection scheduling, and whether the mock-up is sequenced to run in parallel with the main structure or in series.

### Do all hotel brands require a mock-up room?

Most major national hotel brands — including Marriott, Hilton, Hyatt, and IHG — require a brand-approved mock-up room before authorizing full construction to proceed. Brand standards documents for each flag specify the mock-up requirement and inspection process.

### What is the difference between a rough mock-up and a finished mock-up room?

A rough mock-up is built with structural framing exposed, allowing the team to resolve systems placements (electrical, plumbing, backing) before walls close. A finished mock-up applies the full brand prototype — finishes, FF&E, fixtures — and is the stage submitted for brand approval.

### Why is ADA compliance addressed in the mock-up room?

ADA grab bar placement, turning radius, fixture heights, and accessible route requirements are structural decisions that cannot be corrected cost-effectively after framing closes. Resolving them in the rough mock-up stage prevents expensive rework.

### What happens if you skip the mock-up room?

Skipping the mock-up means design errors, brand non-compliance, and ADA issues are discovered after full construction is underway — or during the brand's pre-opening inspection. Corrections at that stage apply to every affected room, multiplying the cost and schedule impact significantly.
