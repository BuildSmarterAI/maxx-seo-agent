## Answer-First Intro

A mock-up room is a single, fully built guest room constructed before the rest of the hotel to test brand standards, finishes, ADA compliance, and FF&E placement. It gives the construction team — and the brand's representative — one room to review and approve so that all downstream rooms replicate a confirmed design rather than an untested one. Catching one wiring error in a mock-up costs a fraction of what it costs across 120 rooms.

## What Is a Mock-Up Room in Hotel Construction?

A mock-up room is the single-room prototype a hotel project team uses to validate design, systems, and brand compliance before replicating those decisions across every guest room in the building. It serves as the physical contract between the owner, the construction manager, and the brand: once approved, it defines the standard every other room must match.

Mock-up rooms matter because hotel projects involve high repetition — often 80 to 300+ identical or near-identical guest rooms. An undetected error in the template multiplies across every room. A mock-up room limits exposure to one room rather than the entire project.

---

## The Two-Stage Mock-Up Process

### Stage 1: Rough Mock-Up Room

The rough mock-up is built with the structural framework exposed. Walls are framed but not closed, which allows the project team to resolve critical systems questions before they are buried behind drywall:

- **Electrical outlet placement and elevation** — verify ADA-compliant heights, convenience outlet clustering, and USB/data locations match the FF&E plan.
- **Plumbing rough-in** — confirm fixture locations align with the room layout and brand requirements.
- **Ceiling light placement** — test spacing, alignment with furniture, and coordination with sprinkler head locations.
- **Backing for wall-mounted items** — TVs, mirrors, artwork, and ADA grab bars all require backing. Rough mock-up is the only cost-effective time to confirm backing location and load rating.
- **3D furniture mockups** — wood-stick or foam-core stand-ins for beds, desks, nightstands, and chairs let the team confirm clearances and traffic flow without committing to actual furniture.

### Stage 2: Finished Mock-Up Room

The finished mock-up applies the full brand prototype package: flooring, wall coverings, paint, FF&E, appliances, window treatments, linens, and artwork. This is the stage where the brand manager or brand representative conducts a formal inspection.

Brand representatives verify:

- Color palettes match the approved brand standard
- FF&E matches the brand's approved product list (substitutions require variance approval)
- Signage, collateral, and brand elements are properly placed
- MEP finishes (light fixtures, outlet covers, HVAC registers) are brand-compliant
- ADA-compliant room elements (grab bars, turning radius, accessible fixtures) meet requirements

Once the brand approves the finished mock-up, the construction team is authorized to replicate the design throughout all guest rooms. The approved mock-up room effectively becomes the job standard — referenced in punchlist reviews, QC walks, and close-out documentation.

## ADA Requirements in Hotel Mock-Up Rooms

ADA compliance in hotel guest rooms is not optional and not easily corrected after framing closes. The rough mock-up stage is the mandatory checkpoint for accessible room elements:

- **Grab bars**: Required at toilet, tub, and shower. Backing must be installed during framing; location and height are regulated under ADA Standards for Accessible Design §§609.
- **Turning radius**: 60-inch clear floor space for wheelchair turns — verify in mock-up before furniture is specified.
- **Accessible route**: Door clear width (minimum 32 inches clear), threshold height, and hardware type must be confirmed.
- **Fixture heights**: Outlets, switches, thermostats, and controls have regulated reach-range limits (15–48 inches AFF).

## Timing: When to Build the Mock-Up

Timing the mock-up is a critical-path decision, not an afterthought.

**Order materials for the mock-up only first.** Do not place full FF&E orders before the mock-up is brand-approved. If the brand requires a substitution or layout change, early bulk ordering creates sunk cost and potential re-procurement delays.

**Build the mock-up during early structural work.** Sequencing the mock-up while the rest of the building is still in structural framing means brand feedback can be incorporated without disrupting the main construction schedule. Maxx Builders sequences mock-up early as a deliberate critical-path strategy on hotel projects.

**Account for international lead times.** FF&E specified from overseas manufacturers (common for brand-standard furniture) carries 12–20+ week lead times depending on origin country, shipping mode, and seasonal factors including national holidays. These lead times must be built into the schedule before the mock-up is started, not after it is approved.

## How Many Mock-Up Rooms Do Hotels Typically Build?

Most hotel projects build one mock-up room per room type offered:

- Standard king
- Double queen
- Extended-stay suite
- ADA accessible room (required in each type)

Room dimensions vary even within the same type. A 150-room hotel may have 60 different king-size rooms with dimensional variations driven by the building's structural grid. Building 2–3 mock-up rooms with representative dimensional variations is enough to establish a design solution that scales to all dimensional variants. Each room type and dimension variant should be documented in updated drawings after mock-up approval.

## FAQ

### What is the purpose of a mock-up room in hotel construction?

A mock-up room is a fully constructed single guest room used to validate design decisions, brand standards, and system placements before those decisions are replicated across the entire hotel. It limits the cost of errors to one room and gives the brand representative a physical room to inspect and approve.

### How long does it take to build a hotel mock-up room?

Most hotel mock-up rooms take 3–5 weeks to construct from rough framing through brand-approved finish. The timeline depends on FF&E lead times, brand inspection scheduling, and whether the mock-up is sequenced to run in parallel with the main structure or in series.

### Do all hotel brands require a mock-up room?

Most major national hotel brands — including Marriott, Hilton, Hyatt, and IHG — require a brand-approved mock-up room before authorizing full construction to proceed. Brand standards documents for each flag specify the mock-up requirement and inspection process.

### What is the difference between a rough mock-up and a finished mock-up room?

A rough mock-up is built with structural framing exposed, allowing the team to resolve systems placements (electrical, plumbing, backing) before walls close. A finished mock-up applies the full brand prototype — finishes, FF&E, fixtures — and is the stage submitted for brand approval.

### Why is ADA compliance addressed in the mock-up room?

ADA grab bar placement, turning radius, fixture heights, and accessible route requirements are structural decisions that cannot be corrected cost-effectively after framing closes. Resolving them in the rough mock-up stage prevents expensive rework.

### What happens if you skip the mock-up room?

Skipping the mock-up means design errors, brand non-compliance, and ADA issues are discovered after full construction is underway — or during the brand's pre-opening inspection. Corrections at that stage apply to every affected room, multiplying the cost and schedule impact significantly.
