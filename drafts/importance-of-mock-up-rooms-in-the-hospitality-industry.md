## What Is a Mock-Up Room in Hotel Construction?

A mock-up room is a prototype guest room or suite assembled to full specification — flooring, wall finishes, millwork, lighting, furniture, fixtures, and equipment (FF&E) — inside the actual building or a dedicated mock-up space before any mass installation begins.

The goal is not aesthetic approval alone. It is a technical and operational review: do the finishes align with brand standards? Does the furniture clear ADA clearance minimums? Is the HVAC register positioned so conditioned air hits the bed? Can a housekeeper navigate the room in a standard turn time?

## Why Do Hotels Build Mock-Up Rooms?

### Is a mock-up room required by hotel brands?

Most major flag brands (full-service, select-service, and extended-stay) require a brand-standard review of a physical mock-up room or a detailed submittals package before approving the full build. Requirements vary by brand tier. **[VERIFY with specific brand program guides before publishing]**

The brand review process typically includes:

| Review Area | What Is Checked |
|---|---|
| FF&E compliance | Furniture dimensions, fabric grades, case goods species |
| Lighting levels | Foot-candles at task, ambient, and accent zones |
| Plumbing fixtures | Flow rates, finish, ADA clearances |
| Signage + wayfinding | Font, placement, ADA compliance |
| Technology | TV placement, USB/outlet locations, Wi-Fi equipment visibility |
| Acoustic performance | Door seals, wall assemblies, HVAC noise |

A single brand deficiency caught at mock-up review costs a rework of one room. The same deficiency caught post-construction costs rework of every room.

### How does a mock-up room reduce change orders?

Change orders are the largest controllable cost risk in hotel construction. Mock-up rooms compress the discovery cycle: every discipline — owner's representative, brand rep, interior designer, MEP engineer, general contractor — walks the space together and resolves conflicts against the built prototype rather than against 2D drawings.

Common issues surfaced at mock-up that drawings miss:

- **Millwork tolerances:** A vanity specified at nominal dimensions may not clear the door swing in the actual framed opening.
- **Lighting shadow zones:** Recessed fixtures that look balanced on a reflected ceiling plan can create glare or dark spots at the desk or bedside.
- **Furniture placement vs. outlet locations:** Nightstand lamps and device-charging outlets that are 18 inches apart on drawings can end up behind a headboard wall.
- **HVAC registers and drape pockets:** Conditioned air from a register positioned too close to a drape pocket creates condensation and mold risk.

Resolving these at one room versus across a full hotel floor drives the ROI case for mock-ups.

### What is the typical timeline for a hotel mock-up room?

| Phase | Typical Duration |
|---|---|
| Mock-up room design and submittals | 4–8 weeks |
| Construction of mock-up space | 2–4 weeks |
| FF&E procurement and installation | 4–6 weeks |
| Brand and ownership review | 1–2 weeks |
| Comment resolution and re-review (if needed) | 1–3 weeks |
| Approved for full-build release | — |

Total: roughly 12–24 weeks ahead of the main construction start. **[VERIFY: align with your actual project schedules]**

Front-loading this time prevents schedule compression during the main construction run, which is where labor costs spike and quality control degrades.

## Who Should Be in the Mock-Up Room Review?

A mock-up review that omits key stakeholders is a mock-up review that will be repeated. The standard review team includes:

1. **Owner / asset manager** — final approval authority on deviations from budget
2. **Brand representative** — confirms compliance with the franchise or flag agreement
3. **Interior designer / architect of record** — verifies design intent is translated correctly
4. **General contractor / project manager** — identifies constructability issues and cost implications of any requested changes
5. **FF&E procurement agent** — flags lead times affected by any substitutions
6. **Operations representative** (GM or Director of Housekeeping) — evaluates guest experience and operational efficiency

The operations representative is often omitted and is almost always the most valuable person in the room. Housekeeping staff navigate the room daily; their input on furniture placement, accessible cleaning paths, and linen storage often prevents post-opening complaints.

## Mock-Up Rooms vs. Virtual Reality Walkthroughs

VR and 3D renderings have improved dramatically, but they do not replace physical mock-ups for several reasons:

| Factor | Physical Mock-Up | VR / Rendering |
|---|---|---|
| Material texture and finish | Exact | Approximated |
| Acoustic performance | Measurable | Not measurable |
| ADA clearance verification | Physical tape measure | Estimable |
| Lighting levels (foot-candles) | Measured with a meter | Color-corrected approximation |
| Smell and air quality | Detectable (off-gassing, HVAC) | Not available |
| Brand approval validity | Accepted by most flags | Not universally accepted |

VR is a useful pre-mock-up tool for concept alignment and ownership preview. It does not substitute for the physical build in brand approval programs or construction document coordination.

## FAQ

### How much does a hotel mock-up room cost?

Mock-up room cost depends on the room type, finish level, and whether FF&E is purpose-built or pulled from the specified production run. For a standard select-service king room, total mock-up costs (construction, FF&E, installation, and review time) commonly range from \$40,000 to \$120,000. **[VERIFY: insert your actual project ranges and footnote the year/scope.]** That figure is weighed against total project FF&E budgets that often run into the millions, making mock-ups one of the highest-ROI line items in pre-construction planning.

### Can you skip the mock-up room to save time?

You can. Most owners who have done it once do not do it again. Skipping the mock-up transfers the discovery cost to the construction phase, where it is compounded by labor disruption, material re-procurement, and schedule compression. The savings are illusory.

### Do all hotel brands require a physical mock-up room?

Requirements vary. Full-service brands under major flags almost universally require it. Some select-service brands accept a detailed submittals package with mock-up photos in lieu of an in-person review. Independent hotels have no external requirement but face the same constructability and design-intent risks. **[VERIFY with the specific brand PIPs your clients work under.]**

### What happens if the mock-up room fails brand review?

The owner and contractor receive a deficiency list with required remediation. The mock-up is corrected and re-reviewed before the full-build release is authorized. A failing mock-up review is not unusual — it is the intended function of the process.

### How is a mock-up room different from a model room?

A mock-up room is a construction and brand-review tool used during pre-construction. A model room is a marketing and sales tool opened to prospective guests or meeting planners after the property opens. The two often overlap — once the mock-up passes review and construction is complete, that room may become the property's model room — but they serve different primary purposes.
