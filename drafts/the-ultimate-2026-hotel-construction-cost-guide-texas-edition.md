## What Are the Main Cost Categories for a Texas Hotel Build?

Every hotel project budget breaks into four buckets: hard costs, soft costs, FF&E/OS&E, and financing/pre-opening.

### Hard Costs

Hard costs cover site work, foundation, structural, MEP (mechanical, electrical, plumbing), and finishes. In Texas, concrete and structural steel costs remain elevated from 2024–2025 tariff cycles. <!-- VERIFY: Confirm current material pricing with your estimating team or a current RS Means Texas supplement. -->

### Soft Costs

Soft costs typically run 20–30% of hard costs and include:
- Architecture and engineering (typically 8–12% of hard costs)
- Permits and impact fees (varies widely by municipality — Austin and Austin-area municipalities have historically added $500K–$2M in fees for larger projects) <!-- VERIFY -->
- Pre-construction and project management
- Legal, title, and financing fees

### FF&E and OS&E

Furniture, Fixtures & Equipment plus Operating Supplies & Equipment add $8,000–$35,000 per key depending on brand and tier. Brand flags (Marriott, Hilton, IHG) impose specific FF&E standards with approved vendor lists. <!-- VERIFY: Confirm per-key FF&E allowances with brand franchise disclosure documents (FDD). -->

### Financing and Pre-Opening

Interest carry during construction, pre-opening staffing, and working capital typically add another 5–10% to total project cost for a standard 24-month build.

## What Drives Hotel Construction Cost Overruns in Texas?

The three most common causes of cost overruns on Texas hotel projects are scope creep, permitting delays, and subcontractor availability gaps.

**Scope creep** is the leading driver. Late changes to room count, brand upgrade requirements, or owner program additions after GMP execution are costly. Lock scope before finalizing the GMP.

**Permitting delays** in high-growth Texas markets (Austin, Round Rock, Kyle) routinely add 3–6 months to project schedules, compounding interest carry.

**Subcontractor depth** is thin in secondary markets. Specialty trades — commercial kitchen, elevator, curtain wall — may require importing crews, which inflates both cost and schedule risk.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does it cost to build a 100-room hotel in Texas?

A 100-room select-service hotel in Texas in 2026 costs approximately $18M–$28M in total project cost, assuming ~65,000 SF of gross floor area, current Texas labor rates, and a standard midscale flag. Urban markets (Austin, DFW) will skew toward the top of that range; secondary markets toward the lower end. <!-- VERIFY: confirm against current Maxx Builders project data. -->

### How long does hotel construction take in Texas?

Most hotel projects in Texas take 18–30 months from permit issuance to certificate of occupancy. Full-service and luxury projects in urban markets can run 30–42 months when permitting time is included. Adding pre-design and entitlement, total development timelines of 36–48 months are common.

### What brand flags are most cost-efficient in Texas?

Select-service flags from Marriott (Fairfield, Courtyard), Hilton (Hampton Inn, Tru), and IHG (Holiday Inn Express) are the most cost-efficient for Texas markets in 2026. Soft brands and independent boutiques can save 5–10% in FF&E by avoiding brand-mandated vendors, but require stronger management infrastructure. <!-- VERIFY: Confirm flag economics with franchise disclosure documents. -->

### What is the difference between hard cost and total project cost?

Hard cost is the construction contract value — labor and materials for the physical structure. Total project cost adds soft costs, FF&E/OS&E, land, financing carry, and pre-opening expenses. For most Texas hotel projects, total project cost runs 1.35–1.55x the hard cost.
