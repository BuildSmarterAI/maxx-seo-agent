## What Does It Cost to Build a Medical Office in Texas in 2026?

Ground-up medical office construction in Texas runs $200–$450 per square foot for hard costs in 2026. The wide range reflects the difference between a simple family-practice clinic and a procedure-heavy specialty suite with imaging rooms, surgical prep areas, or infusion bays.

| Build Type | Hard Cost Range ($/sq ft) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Basic primary care / family practice | $200–$260 | Standard plumbing, minimal procedure rooms |
| Multi-specialty outpatient clinic | $260–$340 | Higher mechanical density, accessible restrooms |
| Surgical / procedure-heavy suite | $340–$450+ | Shielded rooms, medical gas, Class 1 HVAC |
| Tenant improvement (existing shell) | $80–$200 | Depends on base building condition |

Soft costs (design, permitting, FF&E, technology) typically add **20–30% on top** of hard costs. A 5,000 sq ft multi-specialty clinic at $300/sq ft hard costs becomes a $1.6M–$1.95M all-in project before land and financing.

## What Drives Cost in Texas Medical Office Construction?

Medical construction costs more than standard office because of higher system density and regulatory requirements.

**MEP (Mechanical, Electrical, Plumbing)** is the biggest swing factor. Medical facilities require medical-grade HVAC with specific air-exchange rates per ASHRAE 170, often separate air-handling units per zone, and in some cases negative-pressure rooms. In a typical medical office build, MEP runs 30–40% of total hard costs vs. 15–20% in standard commercial office.

**Key cost drivers:**

- **Plumbing density** — clinical sinks at every exam room, scrub stations, medical gas rough-in
- **Structural** — shielded rooms (X-ray, fluoroscopy) require lead lining or concrete mass
- **Accessibility** — Texas requires TAS (Texas Accessibility Standards) compliance, which is stricter than federal ADA on exam room clearances

## How Long Does Medical Office Construction Take in Texas?

Timeline from lease or land control to certificate of occupancy typically runs **12–24 months** for a ground-up medical office in Texas. Tenant improvements run **4–9 months** depending on scope and permit jurisdiction.

**Phase-by-phase breakdown:**

| Phase | Typical Duration |
|---|---|
| Schematic design + programming | 4–8 weeks |
| Construction documents | 8–16 weeks |
| Permitting (city building + health dept) | 6–16 weeks (varies by jurisdiction) |
| Construction | 6–14 months (ground-up) |
| Owner commissioning + inspections | 4–8 weeks |

## What Specialty Types Cost the Most to Build?

Not all medical spaces are created equal. Procedure intensity drives cost more than square footage.

**Highest-cost specialty builds (per sq ft):**

1. **Radiation oncology / linear accelerator vaults** — structural shielding alone can add $500–$1,000+/sq ft to vault areas
2. **Imaging centers (MRI, CT, PET)** — shielding, structural slab reinforcement, RF cage
3. **Ambulatory surgery centers (ASCs)** — HVAC, medical gas, sterile processing
4. **Infusion centers** — IV prep rooms, pharmacy-grade clean areas
5. **Primary care / urgent care** — lowest medical complexity, most cost-comparable to standard office

## FAQ

### How much does it cost to build a 5,000 sq ft medical office in Texas?

A 5,000 sq ft medical office in Texas runs $1.0M–$2.25M in hard construction costs in 2026, depending on specialty type and location. Add 20–30% for soft costs (design, permitting, FF&E, technology) to estimate total project cost.

### Is it cheaper to build or lease medical office space in Texas?

Leasing a shell and doing a tenant improvement is almost always cheaper in year one — $80–$200/sq ft vs. $200–$450/sq ft for ground-up. Build-to-own makes sense when you need a highly customized facility, want long-term cost control, or the local lease market is tight. ### How long does medical office construction take in Texas?

Ground-up: 12–24 months from design kickoff to certificate of occupancy. Tenant improvements: 4–9 months depending on scope and local permitting timelines.

### What is the most expensive part of building a medical office?

MEP systems (HVAC, plumbing, medical gas, electrical) typically represent 30–40% of total hard costs in a medical office build — more in procedure-heavy facilities. This is the primary reason medical construction costs 2–3x standard commercial office.

### Do I need a healthcare-specialized general contractor in Texas?

Not legally required, but strongly advisable. Medical facilities have specific HVAC, plumbing, and infection-control requirements that general commercial contractors often underestimate.
